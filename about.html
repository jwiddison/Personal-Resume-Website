about me

i'm an aspiring web designer and developer

I'm currently working toward a Masters of Information Systems Management at Brigham Young University in Provo, Utah. I love technology and learning how the devices we use every day work. I love good design, and learning about how UI decisions shape the end user's experience with whatever you're developing.

I've grown up working at my Father's small business in the groundwater well service industry. The combined experience I have from studying Information Systems, working in the groundwater industry, and continuing to perform as a jazz musician gives me a unique set of skills and sets me up to be able to make a difference in any field where I end up working.

I've traveled to Europe to play in jazz festivals in Scandavia and the Baltics, I've spent long summer days screwing pipe together and developing groundwater wells, and I learn everything I can about programming and web design.

i'm a jazz musician

I love jazz music. I started playing the alto saxophone at age 11. Since then I've picked up soprano, tenor, and baritone saxophone, flute, piccolo, clarinet, and bass clarinet. I also play the piano and guitar. I love playing jazz for a lot of the same reasons that I love programming , software development, and web design.

In jazz, performers play improvised solos. The approach to playing a good solo is similar to writing a good program. You have a framework of chord changes to play within. Once you understand the rules of that framework, you are free to navigate your way through it in any way you want. It's wonderful to have two outlets for the same type of creativity. Jazz and programming. I really believe that jazz deserves to hold a continuous place in America's popular music.
