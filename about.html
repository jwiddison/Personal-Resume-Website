about me

i'm an aspiring web designer and developer

I'm currently working toward a Masters of Information Systems Management at Brigham Young University in Provo, Utah. I love technology and learning how the devices we use every day work. I love good design, and learning about how our UI design decisions shape the end user's experience with whatever product you're designing.

I've grown up working at my Father's small business in the groundwater-well industry. The combined experience I have from studying Information Systems and working in the groundwater industry gives me a unique set of skills and sets me up to be able to make a difference in any organization where I end up.

I'm also a jazz saxophone player (see below). I have worked hard to be able to compete and perform with any jazz musician at a professional level in the state of utah.

i'm a jazz musician

I love jazz music. I started playing the alto saxophone at age 11. Since then I've picked up soprano, tenor, and baritone saxophone, flute, clarinet, and piano. I love playing jazz for a lot of the same reasons that I love programming, software development, and web design.

In jazz, performers play improvised solos. The approach to playing a good solo is similar to writing a good program. You have a framework of chord changes to play within. Once you understand the rules of that framework, you are free to navigate your way through it in any way you want. It's wonderful to have two outlets for the same type of creativity.

I've traveled to Europe to play in jazz festivals in Scandavia and the Baltics (Including the Võsu jazz festival in Estonia, pictured here), I've spent long summer days screwing pipe together and developing groundwater wells so that they produce more water, completely changed the way that company runs by integrating technology into the company, and I learn everything I can about programming and web design.
